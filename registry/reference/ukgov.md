## ai-safety-report

The first comprehensive international scientific review of AI safety, published January 2025. Written by 100+ independent experts, led by Turing Award winner Yoshua Bengio, and backed by 30 countries.

### Format

```
secid:reference/ukgov/ai-safety-report@2025
```

### Why This Report Matters

- **Largest international collaboration** - 100+ experts from 30 countries
- **Authoritative reference** - Shapes global AI policy debates
- **Led by Yoshua Bengio** - Turing Award winner, UN Scientific Advisory Board member
- **Living document** - Key Updates provide interim findings between annual reports

### Scope

The report provides a shared global picture of:
- Current AI capabilities and limitations
- Risks and potential impacts of general-purpose AI systems
- State of AI safety research and techniques
- Policy implications for governments and organizations

### Backing Organizations

Overseen by an international Expert Advisory Panel including:
- UK Government (commissioning body)
- Representatives from 30 countries invited to AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park
- European Union representatives
- United Nations representatives

### Key Updates

Recognizing rapid AI evolution, Key Updates provide focused interim reports on:
- Major capability breakthroughs since January 2025
- New training techniques enabling more complex problem-solving
- Implications for biological weapons and cyberattack risks
- Challenges for monitoring and controllability
- Growth in Frontier AI Safety Frameworks (doubled since 2025 report)

### Future Reports

- Second International AI Safety Report: Early 2026
- Ahead of AI Impact Summit in India

### Notes

- Published January 2025
- Commissioned by UK Government
- Available at internationalaisafetyreport.org and gov.uk
